The Molecule of Life


In 1998, three scientists were awarded the Nobel Prize in Physiology and Medicine for their work on Nitric Oxide. Called “The Molecule of Life,” Nitric Oxide has been shown to positively influence nearly every system throughout the body.

Research has shown that Nitric Oxide plays a crucial role in fundamental biological processes including the regulation of blood pressure. Nitric Oxide has been shown to expand blood vessels (known as vasodilation), leading to improved circulation, increased energy, and heart health. Nitric Oxide is also important for immune system function and activation mechanisms in the central nervous system that affect everything from gastric motility to memory and behavior. As a signaling molecule, Nitric Oxide has systemic anti-inflammatory effects and improves the storage of information and communication between neurons.

Nitric Oxide helps improve motility and microcirculation, promoting blood flow and nutrient delivery throughout the body. Because Nitric Oxide has been shown to naturally increase blood flow, it is essential to improved sexual libido, performance and satisfaction.
